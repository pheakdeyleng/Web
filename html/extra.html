MPA Program

My journey to BYU Marriott School of Business
1. My work experience

For 8 years I have worked with NGOs in Cambodia. In 2012, I worked for the Cambodian Children’s Fund (CCF) during the day. CCF provides primary school through university educational opportunities for children who scavenge at Steung Meanchey garbage dump on the outskirts of Phnom Penh City. I worked as a Sponsor Relations Officer helping children and parents tell their story to their sponsor and accompanying visiting sponsors to interpret and ensure their safety. When children were injured or went missing, I worked with the CCF community outreach team to gather information and relay that information to sponsors.

2. Translator and interpretor

After graduating from Norton University, I started working for The Cambodia Association of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. In Cambodia, the Church is registered as a small association with only 14 employees. As an administrative assistant I provided support to priesthood leaders and served as a link to government. I wore many hats! For example, I settled all meetinghouse accounts payable, coordinated with priesthood leaders on repair requests, planned deep cleaning at meetinghouses, and scheduled work for building renovations. In addition, because I am bilingual, I regularly interpreted for Cambodia’s Mission President, visiting Area and General Authorities.

As you can see, my experience and work background were in non-profit, so I applied for the MPA Program in 2020.It was during the pandamic and it was hard for me. I was so happy when I was accepted. Now I am at BYU Marriott Business School.
